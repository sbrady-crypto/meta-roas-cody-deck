04Incremental Attribution · Late March · ~4 ROAS points

We lost the ability to choose our attribution window.

Incremental Attribution forces Meta's proprietary "incremental conversions" model. We can't tell the algorithm to optimize against 1-day click, 7-day click, or any combined window like we used to.

ActionWhat we're proposing · open to your input

Three actions.

01

Exclude Audience Network as a placement, account-wide.

Reclaim ~$40K in spend and remove 46.8M low-converting impressions from Meta's training data. The reallocation should immediately tighten CVR on remaining placements.

Why · No tradeoff — we're not actively bidding for this inventory and it's polluting the algorithm's signal.
02

Switch top ASC campaigns to 1-day click + 1-day engaged-view.

Apply to IA Partnership ($928K), IA CostCap ($374K), IA ($358K), and Partnership Winners ($308K) — combined ~$2.0M of after-period spend. These four campaigns make up the largest concentration of click-optimized spend in the account, so the signal recovery will have the highest leverage there first.

Why this window · 1-day engaged-view (3-second views) is exactly the bucket Meta moved out of click attribution. Adding it back into the optimization window pulls those events back into the algorithm's training data and gets us closest to our pre-March setup.
03

Rebalance VO/CO mix — more VO, including for new launches.

Immediate move: shift ASC|CO|IA|CreativeTesting (+$62K, new) back to VO. It's not showing improvement under CO, and creative testing runs more efficiently under VO. Going forward, we need to be more disciplined for launches — we've been defaulting to CO without a strong reason, and VO is the better starting point given its 2.2× ROAS premium.

Why · VO continues to materially outperform CO on ROAS (1.53 vs 0.71). The discipline question is when CO is actually the right call — not the default.

Bottom line

The drop is real. But it's mostly measurement and mix — not demand.

Of the 26 reported ROAS points lost, ~16–20 are measurement reshuffle and structural overcorrection we can recover. Real residual decline is closer to ~6–10 points — solvable with the four actions.
